About this tool

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a quick screening measure that relates your weight to your height. It's widely used as a general indicator, though it doesn't account for muscle mass, bone density, age or sex.

This calculator works entirely client-side — enter your numbers and the standard BMI formula runs instantly in your browser.

How to use the bmi calculator

  1. Pick metric (cm / kg) or imperial (in / lb) units.
  2. Enter your height and weight.
  3. Your BMI and weight category appear instantly below.

Frequently asked questions

What is the BMI formula?

Metric: weight (kg) ÷ height (m)². Imperial: 703 × weight (lb) ÷ height (in)².

Is BMI accurate for everyone?

BMI is a useful general screening tool but doesn't distinguish muscle from fat, so it can be less accurate for athletes, older adults, or during pregnancy. Use it as a starting point, not a diagnosis.

What are the BMI categories?

Under 18.5 is underweight, 18.5–24.9 is a healthy weight, 25–29.9 is overweight, and 30 or above is considered obese, per WHO guidelines.
