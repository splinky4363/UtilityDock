About this tool

This tool encodes whatever text you enter into a scannable QR code image, right in your browser, using the small open-source “qrcode” library loaded from a CDN.

It's perfect for sharing links, Wi-Fi passwords, contact details or short messages that people can scan with a phone camera.

How to use the qr code generator

  1. Type or paste any text, link, Wi-Fi detail or message.
  2. Pick a size and click “Generate QR code”.
  3. Download it as a PNG or copy the image straight into another app.

Frequently asked questions

Does the QR code expire?

No. The QR code simply encodes the text you provide — there's no tracking, shortening or expiry involved.

What size should I use for print?

For printed materials, generate at 320px or larger to keep the code easy to scan from a normal reading distance.

Can I use this for Wi-Fi or contact cards?

Yes — paste a properly formatted Wi-Fi (WIFI:S:name;T:WPA;P:password;;) or vCard string and most phone cameras will recognise it.
