About this tool

The percentage calculator covers the three percentage questions people search for most: finding a percentage of a number, working out what percentage one number is of another, and measuring the percentage increase or decrease between two values.

All maths happens locally in your browser using plain JavaScript, so results appear instantly and nothing you type ever leaves your device.

How to use the percentage calculator

  1. Choose a calculation mode: “X% of Y”, “X is what % of Y”, or “% change”.
  2. Enter the two numbers the mode asks for.
  3. Read the result instantly — it updates as you type, no button needed.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate a discount?

Use “X% of Y” with the discount percentage and the original price to find the amount taken off, then subtract it from the price yourself, or use the % change mode with the original and discounted price to see the percentage drop.

What is the formula for percentage increase?

Percentage increase = ((new value − original value) ÷ original value) × 100. The “% change” mode does this automatically and tells you whether it's an increase or decrease.

Can I use negative or decimal numbers?

Yes, all three modes accept decimals and negative numbers.
